# Thoughts On How To Build Better Together

These documents represent years of accumulated thinking about how to build great software and the teams that make it. They are a mixture of hard-won lessons, ideas borrowed and adapted from people smarter than me, and principles that have I've put into practice with some success. Not much of it is original, good ideas rarely are. But I've tried to synthesize what has actually worked in practice and give credit where it's due.

This is a living document. My thinking continues to evolve, and I expect it always will. If something I've written resonates with you, challenges you, or strikes you as flat-out wrong, I'd genuinely love to hear about it. Feedback and pushback are how these ideas get sharper.

My hope is that some of what's here is useful to others trying to build high-functioning, high-trust engineering teams, and that it saves someone a few of the harder lessons I had to learn firsthand.
