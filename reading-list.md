

## Recommended Reading

| Title | Author | Why It's Worth Reading |
|---|---|---|
| [The Phoenix Project](https://itrevolution.com/product/the-phoenix-project/) | Gene Kim, Kevin Behr, George Spafford | Story-driven introduction to the Theory of Constraints applied to software delivery. If you prefer non-fiction, the source material is [The Goal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Goal_(novel)) by Eliyahu Goldratt. |
| [Design It!](https://pragprog.com/titles/mkdsa/design-it/) | Michael Keeling | Near-essential reading on software architecture. |
| [Release It!](https://pragprog.com/titles/mnee2/release-it-second-edition/) | Michael Nygard | Near-essential reading on designing and operating resilient production systems. |
| [Accelerate](https://www.simonandschuster.ca/books/Accelerate/Nicole-Forsgren-PhD/9781942788331) | Nicole Forsgren, Jez Humble, Gene Kim | Execellent insights into the qualities that actually produce high performing engineering organizations. |
| [The Manager's Path](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/the-managers-path/9781491973882/) | Camille Fournier | Valuable for everyone building software products; essential reading if you want to grow into management and leadership. |
| [7 Habits of Highly Effective People](https://www.amazon.com/Habits-Highly-Effective-People-Powerful/dp/1982137274) | Stephen R. Covey | Clichéd for a reason. A durable framework for setting and enacting goals and building sustainable behavior patterns. |
